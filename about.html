15 Years of Excellence

From the Founder

A Brand Born From Frustration

In 2010, I walked into store after store looking for a shirt that fit well, felt premium, and would last more than a season. I found nothing. Either the quality was there but the fit wasn't, or the price was absurd with nothing to justify it.

So I started MANOR out of a small studio in New York with one simple rule: make clothes I would actually wear. No shortcuts on fabric. No compromises on construction. No inflated brand tax.

Fifteen years later, we've grown from a one-room operation to a team of 80 people across three continents — but that rule has never changed. Every piece we make goes through the same rigorous process. Every fabric is chosen by hand. Every stitch is accountable to the man who will wear it.

Daniel Hargrove
Founder & Creative Director, MANOR

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

The principles that guide every decision we make

🧶

Uncompromising Quality

We source only the finest materials — Supima cotton, Italian wool, Portuguese linen. Every fabric passes a 14-point quality check before a single stitch is made. We'd rather discontinue a line than lower our standards.

⚙

Honest Craftsmanship

Our garments are made in family-owned factories in Portugal and Italy, where artisans with decades of experience bring each piece to life. We visit every partner factory in person, every year — no exceptions.

🌿

Responsible Fashion

Fast fashion is not our model. We design for longevity, not for landfill. Every MANOR product is made to last years, not seasons. We also offset 100% of our shipping emissions and use recycled packaging exclusively.

📍

Radical Transparency

We publish our full supply chain — who makes what, where, and how much we pay them. You deserve to know where your clothes come from. No hidden factories, no greenwashing.

👤

Customer First, Always

Free returns, lifetime alterations, and a 365-day satisfaction guarantee. If a MANOR piece ever fails to meet your expectations, we make it right — no questions asked. This is our promise.

🌎

Global Community

From New York to Tokyo, Lagos to Dubai — MANOR belongs to men everywhere. We celebrate diversity in style and culture, and we design pieces that transcend borders and speak a universal language of elegance.

How We Got Here

Our Journey

Fifteen years of growth, learning, and relentless improvement

2010
The First Shirt
Daniel Hargrove launches MANOR from a 400 sq ft studio in Manhattan, selling hand-crafted Oxford shirts directly to customers. First 100 shirts sell out in 48 hours.
2012
First Factory Partnership
We partner with the Mendes family's 3rd-generation atelier in Porto, Portugal. This partnership still powers over 60% of our production today. Quality becomes our defining trait.
2014
100,000 Customers
MANOR crosses the 100,000 customer milestone and expands into trousers, knitwear, and outerwear. Opens first physical showroom in New York's Soho neighborhood.
2017
Sustainability Pledge
MANOR becomes one of the first menswear brands to publish a full supply chain transparency report. We commit to carbon-neutral shipping and 100% recycled packaging by 2020 — and deliver 8 months early.
2020
Global Expansion
Despite global challenges, MANOR launches international shipping to 50+ countries and surpasses $50M in annual revenue. The team grows to 80 people across 4 offices worldwide.
2025
The Next Chapter
With 500,000 customers across 50 countries, MANOR launches its most ambitious collection yet — and continues to push the boundaries of what premium menswear can be.

Our Commitment

Fashion That Respects the Planet

Sustainability isn't a marketing term for us — it's how we operate

♻
100% Recycled Packaging
Every box, bag, tissue paper, and label we use is made from post-consumer recycled materials. We eliminated virgin plastic from our supply chain entirely in 2020.
🌏
Carbon-Neutral Shipping
We offset 100% of the carbon emissions generated by every shipment, worldwide. We partner with verified reforestation projects in Brazil, Kenya, and Indonesia.
👷
Fair Wages, Always
Every factory worker in our supply chain earns at least 150% of the local living wage. We audit all partner factories annually — unannounced — and publish the results publicly.
🌿
Low-Impact Fabrics
We prioritize natural, renewable, and low-impact materials. 85% of our collection uses certified organic cotton, recycled wool, or TENCEL™ lyocell. We're targeting 100% by 2026.
